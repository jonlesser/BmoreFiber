Leader in Serious Games

Image: Breakaway Games: Patient in ICU

"Serious Games" use video game technology to train healthcare providers, first-responders, and soldiers. With Google Fiber, Baltimore will make these data-intensive environments more immersive and effective, ultimately saving lives.

Healthcare providers, first responders, and soldiers must make tough decisions quickly and under pressure, requiring situational awareness and acuity. "Serious games" are interactive digital simulations that provide immersive virtual learning environments that can help train for these skills in a controlled setting.

In healthcare, clinical training increasingly requires the use of immersive new technology. Using game-based learning platforms, students can experience everything from point-of-injury to release in lifelike virtual environments, with dynamic virtual patients for medical personnel. First responders can experience real-world emergencies with life-like graphics and sound. Game-based simulations permit on-demand, discovery-based, learning of crucial expertise in controlled settings. Users can practice and refine an array of skills, including critical thinking, decision-making and differential diagnostics.

The modern strategy gaming industry arguably got its start in Baltimore with Avalon Hill Games in 1958, followed by such companies as Microprose (and award-winning games like Sid Meier's Civilization), Big Huge Games, and Firaxis. These companies, together with an academic center of excellence, the University of Baltimore Simulation and Digital Entertainment Program; and Federal customers in Maryland and Washington, D.C., have made the Baltimore area an undisputed leader in recreational and defense simulation game development. Breakaway Games produces the medical training platform "vHealthCare", which simulates ICU cases based on Advanced Trauma Life Support (ATLS) standards developed by the American College of Surgeons. Breakaway also created Incident Commander, a 2D game-based National Incident Management System training program distributed by the Department of Justice to over 50,000 municipalities across the United States.

With Google Fiber, Baltimore will extend its leadership in serious game development to make data-intensive, 3D-stereo immersive learning environments and multiplayer crisis simulations even more realistic and ubiquitous.
